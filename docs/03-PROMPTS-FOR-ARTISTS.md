# Prompts for artists

Copy, paste, adjust. Mood words do more than measurements.

## Building

- *Build a small campfire clearing: dirt circle, ring of uneven stones, two
  fallen logs to sit on, warm flickering firelight. Evening, slightly misty.*
- *Blockout a cozy reading nook interior: one window with strong light coming
  through, dust in the air, a chair, shelves. Keep everything grayboxed except
  the light.*
- *Scatter 30 rocks of varied size along the slope, denser at the bottom,
  like they rolled there. No two rocks the same rotation.*
- *Give me three lighting moods for this same scene, one after another:
  golden hour, overcast morning, and blue night with one practical light.
  Screenshot each so I can compare.*
- *Make a 100m dirt road that curves gently left, with wooden fence posts
  every few meters on the right side only.*

## Art direction (after it builds)

- *Warmer.* / *Moodier.* / *Less symmetrical — nature isn't tidy.*
- *The fog is too thick, I want depth, not soup.*
- *Pull the camera to knee height and re-shoot; I want it to feel small.*
- *Everything is too evenly spaced. Clump things, leave gaps.*

## Understanding a project

- */read-blueprint* — then pick from the list.
- *What does BP_DoorSliding do and which of its settings would change how it
  feels, not how it works?*
- *List every Blueprint in this project and sort them into: player stuff,
  environment stuff, UI, and mystery.*
- */check-scene — what would a level designer complain about?*
- *This marketplace pack came with 40 blueprints. Which three should I
  actually learn to use?*
- */what-is a material instance — and why does everyone tell me to use them?*

## Learning while doing

- *Do it, but narrate: tell me which tool you're using at each step and why,
  so I learn where things live in Unreal.*
- *Show me two ways to get this look — the quick hack and the proper way —
  and tell me when the hack will bite me.*
- *Quiz me: show me the scene, let me guess what's wrong with the lighting,
  then tell me if I'm right.*

## Safety valves

- *Before you change anything: what exactly are you about to do?*
- *Undo everything you added since the last screenshot.*
- *Don't save anything — I want to look first.*
